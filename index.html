Gas Leakage Detector

The gas leakage detector project is a simple but efficient device designed to detect and alert the presence of gas leaks. The device uses an MQ-5 gas sensor that detects the presence of a variety of gases including methane, propane, and LPG. The device incorporates an Arduino Uno board, which processes data from the sensor and triggers an alarm when gas is detected. The alarm can be in the form of a buzzer, LED, or a notification in a smartphone application.During the project, I gained experience in embedded programming, electronic circuit design, and sensor integration. It also helped me understand the importance of safety in the workplace and the role of technology in enhancing safety measures.

Drowsiness Detection system for drivers

The project aimed to reduce the risk of accidents caused by driver fatigue, the leading cause of fatal crashes on highways. We conducted extensive research, collected data on driver behaviors, and implemented computer vision algorithms to identify signs of drowsiness, such as drooping eyelids and head nodding. We used deep learning models like Convolutional Neural Networks to classify these patterns and alert drivers in real-time if they showed signs of fatigue. We integrated the AI system into a user-friendly mobile application that alerts drivers to take a break or stop driving if they show signs of drowsiness.
